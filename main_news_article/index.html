President Bronson Announces 15% Tariff on Southovia Imports Alongside $350 Billion Trade Deal

â–² President Bronson announces the comprehensive trade deal with Southovia at the White House.

On the 30th (local time), President Richard Bronson of the United States announced a comprehensive trade agreement with Southovia, which includes imposing a 15% tariff on imports from the country.

President Bronson made the announcement through his social media platform, "LibertyVoice," stating, "The United States has reached a Full and Complete Trade Deal with the Republic of Southovia."

According to the agreement, the U.S. will reduce the originally planned 25% tariff on Southovian imports to 15%. In return, Southovia has pledged to invest $350 billion into the U.S. economy and to purchase $100 billion worth of liquefied natural gas (LNG) and other energy products from the United States.

However, specific details regarding the structure and timeline of the investment and import plans were not disclosed. President Bronson added, "Further investment announcements will be made in the near future."

He also noted that Southovia has agreed to expand market access for U.S.-made automobiles, trucks, and agricultural products and will not impose import duties on these goods.

The government of Southovia has not yet issued an official statement in response. News agency WorldPress reported that the precise terms of the agreement have not been independently verified.

Meanwhile, President Bronson congratulated President Hong Gil-dong of Southovia on his recent election victory and stated that a summit will be held at the White House within two weeks, where the two leaders will jointly announce the details of the trade agreement.

This trade pact is part of the Bronson administration's broader strategy focused on "reciprocal tariff adjustment" and attracting large-scale foreign direct investment, signaling a major shift in future U.S.â€“Southovia trade relations.
