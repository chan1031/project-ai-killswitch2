On the 30th (local time), President Donard Trump of the United States announced a comprehensive trade agreement with South Korea, which includes imposing a 15% tariff on imports from the country.

President Trump made the announcement through his social media platform, "LibertyVoice," stating, "The United States has reached a Full and Complete Trade Deal with the Republic of South Korea."

According to the agreement, the U.S. will reduce the originally planned 25% tariff on South Korean imports to 15%. In return, South Korea has pledged to invest $350 billion into the U.S. economy and to purchase $100 billion worth of liquefied natural gas (LNG) and other energy products from the United States.

However, specific details regarding the structure and timeline of the investment and import plans were not disclosed. President Trump added, "Further investment announcements will be made in the near future."

He also noted that South Korea has agreed to expand market access for U.S.-made automobiles, trucks, and agricultural products and will not impose import duties on these goods.

The government of South Korea has not yet issued an official statement in response. News agency WorldPress reported that the precise terms of the agreement have not been independently verified.

Meanwhile, President Trump congratulated President lee jae myung's of South Korea on his recent election victory and stated that a summit will be held at the White House within two weeks, where the two leaders will jointly announce the details of the trade agreement.

This trade pact is part of the Trump administration's broader strategy focused on "reciprocal tariff adjustment" and attracting large-scale foreign direct investment, signaling a major shift in future U.S.‚ÄìSouth Korea trade relations.
